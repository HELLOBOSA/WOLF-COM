Horizon House

Located on a Spanish coastal site, Horizon House is a single-family residence that seeks to merge with the surrounding landscape through a restrained, luminous, and precise architecture.

Wolfblanc Architects — Sanxenxo, Galicia, Spain

Photos© Wolfblanc Architects

Located on a Spanish coastal site, Horizon House is a single-family residence that seeks to merge with the surrounding landscape through a restrained, luminous, and precise architecture. The project is defined by a matte charcoal steel structure and warm beige plaster walls that frame the horizon, creating a seamless continuity between interior and exterior spaces.

The program unfolds on a single level, where the living areas, kitchen, and bedroom are aligned toward the sea, extending onto a generous perimeter terrace. Natural light, structural rhythm, and transparency shape a serene atmosphere in which every room opens to the landscape while maintaining a sense of shelter and calm.

True to Wolfblanc Architects's philosophy, the project combines an essential aesthetic with meticulous attention to construction detail and the dialogue between material and context. Horizon House is a reflection on simplicity, proportion, and the intimate connection between architecture and nature.
