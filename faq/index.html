Fees depend on project type, scope and stage of involvement. Permits & regulations

Most works beyond basic maintenance require some form of permit. In Spain the type depends on scale — comunicado, licencia de obras menores or licencia de obras mayores. We assess what's needed as part of our feasibility work and manage the full permit process on your behalf.

A due diligence inspection is a structured technical assessment of a property before purchase. We review the structure, installations, planning status, existing permits, and potential hidden costs, and produce a report that gives you a clear picture of what you're buying and what it would cost to bring it to the standard you need. It typically takes 1–2 weeks from inspection to report.

Technical & credentials

Yes. Wolfblanc holds professional registration in Sweden (SAR/MSA), Spain (COAM — Colegio Oficial de Arquitectos de Madrid) and Greece (TEE-TCG). We also hold WELL AP accreditation. This means we can sign and submit building permits and construction projects in all three countries.

BIM (Building Information Modelling) is a methodology for producing and managing building information in a coordinated 3D model. It reduces clashes between structure, architecture and MEP, produces higher-quality construction documentation, and helps control costs. For projects above roughly 500 m² or with complex MEP, BIM typically saves more than it costs. For smaller residential projects the value is more about coordination quality than cost savings.

WELL AP (Accredited Professional) is a credential for practitioners trained in the WELL Building Standard, which focuses on how buildings affect occupant health and wellbeing — air quality, light, acoustics, thermal comfort, and physical activity. Wolfblanc holds WELL AP accreditation and integrates these principles into our residential, workplace and hospitality projects.
